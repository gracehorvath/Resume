MasterCard

Java Developer Intern Summer 2014, St. Louis

I interned on the MasterPass team in the Emerging Payments department as a Java developer. MasterPass is a digital wallet that allows consumers to store credit card and shipping information to create a seamless shopping experience. At the time, MasterPass was migrating from a single authorization web service to two new services, one for the business and the other for the consumer. I spent most of the summer making a mocked service API that simulated the consumer authorization web service to allow developers to use this service on their local machines. I then moved on to creating an adapter class that would map the calls made from the merchant and the consumer to the appropriate authorization web service.

TEALS

2016-2017 School Year

Technology Education and Literacy in Schools (TEALS) is an organization sponsored by Microsoft that aims to educate teachers, alongside students, computer science in order to create a sustainable CS program. I was placed on a team of four volunteers and one classroom teacher at Austin Achieve High School. We each spend two to three days a week in the classroom teaching Intro to Computer Science to 9th graders. We are using TEALS's curriculum to teach twenty-five students the fundamentals of CS. We have began the year with Berkeley's Snap programming language and will progress to Python. The students are tracked and monitored by labs, projects, quizzes, and feedback is provided to students to help further their understanding.
